Consequences of Environmentally Harmful Activities

Waste

Modern human civilization carries an inherent environmental burden, requiring significant quanitities of natural resources to function, but humanity actually consumes much more than necessary. Humans consume much more than necessary because many resources are simply wasted. This behavior unncessarily balloons humanity's environmental footprint.

Waste Can Be Classified Into Two Categories:

The Act of Wasting

Humans waste several natural resources, both through direct waste of the resources themselves and waste of the human-usable resources generated from the raw ones.

Natural Artificial Means of Waste
Coal, gas, oil, etc. Electricity Leaving lights/HVAC on, running appliances frequently
Fresh water Treated water Long showers, leaving sinks running, leaks
Crops and livestock Food Leaving food in refrigerator to spoil, large restaurant portions
