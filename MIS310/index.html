Save Our Beautiful Earth

It cannot wait

Human civilization is currently unsustainable. Modern human activities result in deforestation, pollution, loss of species, climate change, and other serious threats to our planet. Should the status quo remain, biodiversity will plummet, natural resources will be much less clean, and Earth will become less habitable overall, both for human and non-human life.

Why is Earth harmed?

It is more productive to focus on the root causes behind environmentally destructive behaviors than on the behaviors themselves. Quick fixes like replanting trees and reducing consumption of meat cannot hurt, but they fail to poke at the systems and institutions at the root of unsustainable activities. These root causes include but are not limited to:

Greed and Abuse of Power

Environmentally harmful activities benefit wealthy, powerful entities like politicians and corporations but not the masses. The fossil fuel and oil industries, for example, not only are very profitable but lobby the government to cement additional power. Also, wealthier nations account for most environmental harm, yet less wealthy nations suffer disproportionate impacts.

Inertia

A harsh reality of humans is their resistance to change. Modern civilization has gotten so used to unsustainable routines that to change them would be very uncomfortable. But there's more to it: more sustainable forms of energy, food, and transportation carry drawbacks and societal costs.

Consumer Behavior

Buyers worsen environmental woes by selecting unsustainable products. They may be cheap and/or convenient but are what allow the planet-destroying companies to make money. General overconsumption and waste also do not help.

Overpopulation

Human society inherently competes with Mother Nature. The eight billion humans on Earth each require food, shelter, power, and water. A transition to a more sustainable planet will become even more challenging as the global population continues to soar.

How can Earth heal?

All governments, businesses, and individuals can choose a more sustainable future. They must stand against the status quo and embrace a new world order. While it is not too late to take action, any further delay will only result in additional needless suffering and catastrophe.

Market Regulation

Many environmentally damaging activities are linked to mega-corporations. While the free market must be preserved, stricter regulations on planet-taxing industrial processes are worth a shot.

Budget Reallocation

Money is one of the most powerful impediments to meaningful policymaking. Many oppose climate action due to a surge in spending, but there are plenty of lower-priority sectors from which monies could be redirected.

Education

Those who downplay environmental threats may simply be ignorant. Schools must teach and model sustainable practices from kindergarten through 12th grade. Children must also learn to think critically and be aware that some "research" is simply industry-funded propaganda.

Personal Changes

While a single person's actions only negligibly impact the planet, one's eco-friendly lifestyle modifications can encourage others to get on board. In the age of social media, it is incredibly easy for one individual to influence thousands or even millions of other people.
