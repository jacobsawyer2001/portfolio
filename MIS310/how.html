How Can Earth Heal?

Market Regulation

Major corporations are among the largest culprits of environmental damage. Environmental action should focus on holding them accountable to the people. Action and regulation must also be detailed and comprehensive to prevent businesses from fudging the facts.

Indirect emissions

Many well-known brands have pledged to reduce emissions, but some exclude indirect emissions, such as those generated by suppliers who deliver unsustainably produced materials. These emissions can account for 70% or more of a company's total.

Emissions can be classified into three categories or "scopes". Scope 3 is the indirect, underreported one.

Scope 1 Scope 2 Scope 3
Direct; industrial processes Indirect; energy consumption Indirect: external to but still influenced by corp.

Governments should encourage Scope 3 emission reductions by:

Tightening reporting laws

Taxing disposable items/packaging

Subsidizing recycling

Investing in renewable energy

Positive Reinforcement

Government regulation should not be limited to fines, lawsuits, and other punitive measures. Offering incentives for compliance would help maintain free-market capitalism and reduce prices on goods from name brands truly committed to sustainability. This could be very helpful in regulating the food industry, as the cheapest companies tend to engage in environmentally destructive practices.

Subsidize: Tax:
Local produce International produce
Nuts, seeds, soya Beef and cheese*
Organic items Ultra-processed items
Fairtrade dark chocolate Non-Fairtrade chocolate

*NOTE: Grass-fed animal products are considerably healthier than their grain-fed, cheaper cousins, but both types contribute to deforestation and methane gas emissions.

Budget Reallocation

Governments' spending capacities are finite, and sweeping environmental reforms carry high upfront price tags. With a little open-mindedness, though, world leaders can find ways to offset those upfront costs without tanking the economy or worsening national debts.

Defund war

Modern armed conflicts are driven mainly by the interests of politicians and corporations as opposed to those of the people. Yet military spending accounts for roughly 3% of U.S. GDP. While that number may seem trivial, the sheer size of U.S. GDP means reducing it could free up valuable funds for climate action.

Modest Green New Deal

Investments in infrastructure like high-speed rail and nuclear power could significantly reduce CO2 emissions without the level of risk and uncertainty more ambitious proposals carry. Contrary to what both the left wing and right wing claim, the world can balance systemic change with preservation of economic liberty.

Do: Do NOT:
Embrace nuclear power. Aim for immediate 100% wind/solar power.
Use parking lots for solar energy. Clear more forests for wind/solar.
Replant forests. Rely on carbon capture (energy-intensive).
Foster technological innovation. Aim to create a utopia.
Hold huge corporations accountable. Neglect small businesses.

Education

Schools should implement honest, politically unbiased curricula on climate change and sustainability. Ignorance breeds polarization, while open-mindedness and critical thinking breed collaboration.

Tell

Adult viewpoints and personalities tend to be rooted in early childhood. Experiences in later childhood and adolescence reinforce some of these beliefs and challenge others. Grade-level-specific curricula would help instill within students respect for the planet:

Grade level Teach/demonstrate:
Pre-K Cleaning up, following rules, displaying recycle bins
Elementary Recycling, turning lights/water off, respecting animals/nature
Middle Environmental threats, media literacy, ecosystems
High Recognizing quackery, sustainable lifestyle choices, scholarly research
All levels Mandatory recycling, reusable food containers, transparency (i.e. does the recycle actually get recycled?).
Show

Actions speak louder than words. Schools will have a hard time teaching environmentally friendly behaviors if students continue to notice trash cans full of styrofoam trays and plastic bottles. Schools must mean what they say and can do so by:

Fresher meals

Solar panels

Composting/gardening programs

Electric school buses

Personal Changes

While there is not much difference any one person can make, the actions of many environmentally conscious individuals add up. When more people demand sustainability, businesses and politicians will need to modify their behavior to satisfy those demands.

Vote with your wallet

Environmentally-conscious brands and products tend to be more expensive than private-label items, but they really do make a difference. It's just like donating to a climate charity--except you're getting a high-quality item in return.
